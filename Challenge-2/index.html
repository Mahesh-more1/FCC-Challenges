Marie Curie

Marie Curie (1867-1934) was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person and only woman to win the Nobel Prize twice, and the only person to win the Nobel Prize in two different scientific fields.

Her achievements included:

  • Development of the theory of radioactivity (a term she coined)
  • Techniques for isolating radioactive isotopes
  • Discovery of two elements, polonium and radium
  • First studies into the treatment of neoplasms using radioactive isotopes
  • Founding the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw
